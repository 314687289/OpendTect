4.2. Initial Model Building

Purpose: To construct a 3D Model that will be suitable input for Deterministic Inversion or Stochastic Inversion. This workflow can only be applied to 3D data.

Theory: Deterministic Inversion and Stochastic Inversion both require a 3D Model (and 2D Error Grid) as input. These models can be created entirely within the Earthworks MPSI plugin using the EW3DModelBuilder attribute or pre-constructed within OpendTect as a HorizonCube-based seismic volume. Both routes to construction require well and horizon data.

Software: OpendTect + HorizonCube

Workflow:

1. Perform your Well-Tie (Analysis - Wells - Tie Well to Seismic...)

2. Choose the Horizons you wish to use. These Horizons may be created from your own interpretation or derived from a Steering Cube. There are several post-processing considerations:

o The Horizons should be without holes - close any holes using the Gridding option.

o Ensure that the bin size and area size match that of the seismic data.

o If you wish to perform any filtering on your Horizons, then now is the time to do so.

o Check Horizons for crossing.

3. Create your HorizonCube.

4. HorizonCube-Well Interpolation. This step consists of two stages: you need to create two output quantities, one for Sonic and one for Density. Use the 'Inverse Distance' Algorithm.

5. Using Mathematics, create Velocity and Acoustic Impedance attributes. Please take care of your units.

6. (Optional) Apply a Frequency Filter Attribute.

7. Process the Velocity and Acoustice Impedance attributes as volumes.

8. Export these volumes as SEG-Y file.

Tips:

1. In step 1., if you wish to extend your well logs, this should be done as an initial step, prior to the well tie, using the log creator (Log Extension).

2. In step 2., Gridding can be repeated as necessary until you are satisfied with the result.

3. In step 3., a model-driven HorizonCube is usually sufficient, though a data-driven HorizonCube is more detailed and may be preferable. The choice is yours.

4. In step 4., use the 'Inverse Distance' Algorithm.

5. In step 5., ensure that you take care to correct the units. You can do this directly thus:

Let Velocity = 1e6 / Sonic

and

let AI = 1e6 * Density / Sonic

. You may also wish to apply filters at this point. For example, the Horizon-based Painter, Body-Painter and Smoother are available in the Volume Builder. Alternatively, you may wish to use theMathematics attribute to clip and/or compress the data, ie:

vel < c0 ? c0 : vel

6. In step 6., you can, for example, use the FFT LowPass with max frequency set to an appropriate level.
